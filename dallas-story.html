Chapter One

He Just
Appeared.

No warning. No backstory. Just a tabby cat who materialized in the yard one day like the universe had placed an order and forgotten to tell us.

Brad and I thought he was a she. We thought she was older. We started putting food out — because that's what you do — with the quiet plan to trap and spay and maybe find a home.

We had no idea what we were starting.

The Ring cameras caught him first: climbing the fence at dusk, moving through the yard with the careful, measured steps of a cat who trusted nothing and no one. He ate fast. He disappeared faster. He was a ghost with good bone structure.

And then he kept coming back.

"We thought we were feeding a stray. We were actually being chosen."

First sighting. Fence cam. Dusk. He had no idea what was coming.
Flopped in the yard. Already comfortable. Zero shame.
Chapter Two

He Tried To
Murder Me.

We trapped him. We got him neutered. And then — because I am apparently incapable of reasonable self-preservation — I decided I was going to make friends with him.

Dallas had opinions about this.

Strong ones. Delivered with claws. Repeatedly. Across my hands, my wrists, my arms. Documented from multiple angles because if you're going to suffer for a cat, you might as well have proof.

The medical verdict: Cat Scratch Fever. An actual bacterial infection. Actual antibiotics. Actual doctor visit. All because I was determined and Dallas was not ready and neither of us was willing to back down first.

Most people would have stopped here. I want you to know that I was not most people. I was someone with a plan and a raptor glove and entirely too much faith in the process.

⚠ Exhibit B — The Basement Incident

There was a day in the basement when I pushed too far. Dallas caught a vein. An 8-inch pool of blood. I called Brad from my cell: "Can you bring me the first aid kit?" He sighed. He thought I was being dramatic. He came downstairs.

He was not prepared for what he found.

My priority: "Shhhh — you're going to scare him more. He's under the bookcase."
Brad's priority: the woman bleeding on the basement floor.
Dallas's priority: not his problem. He was grooming himself.

Brad cleaned up the crime scene. Later that night, lying in the dark:

"Hey Brad?"

"Yes?"

"I just thought of something. I better never go missing. Because if that basement ever gets luminol... you are straight to prison."

Crickets.

"Woman. Your Dateline watching days are about to be banned."

We kept watching Dateline. Dallas was not sorry. Brad has since accepted his fate. 🐾

Chapter Four

The Paw
Reached Out.
Everything Changed.

It happened slowly and then all at once — the way the best things always do.

The blanket got looser. His body got softer. The ears stopped pinning back when I reached for him. And then one day, instead of tensing up — he extended a paw toward my hand.

Just that. Just a paw. But it was everything.

Then came the first play session — him on his hind legs chasing a green feather wand, completely uninhibited, ears forward, not a trace of fear. A kitten finally being a kitten.

And then, a few days after that: I lost custody of my face. He climbed onto my chest, pressed his entire head against my cheek, and just... stayed there. Like he'd always meant to. Like the raptor gloves and the back scratcher and the guest room were just the price of admission to this exact moment.

Worth it. Every single time.

"He didn't just learn to trust me. He decided I was his. There's a difference."

The paw. The moment. The whole story.
First play session. Pure joy. No fear.
Chapter Five

I Lost Custody
Of My Face.
And I'd Do It Again.

That's the only way to describe it. One day he was a cat I had to use a stick to touch. The next, he was draped across my actual face like he'd been doing it his whole life.

He chose my chest as his personal real estate. My cheek as his pillow. My presence as his anchor. And the smile on my face in those photos — that's what months of raptor gloves and antibiotics and guest room floor sleeping looks like when it pays off.

He followed me to work. Stood on his hind legs at doors asking to be where I was. Turned from a cat who hid in built-in shelves to a cat who needed to be in the same room at all times.

He decided I was his person. And that decision? That's not something you can force or earn or rush. You can only be patient enough to be there when it happens.

Face custody: transferred. He is not giving it back.

Dallas Now

Perfect Paws.
Blue Bow Tie. Distinguished.

Dallas sat down one day with his paws perfectly square, back perfectly straight, expression perfectly composed, and looked directly at me like he had always been exactly this dignified and I was the one who had failed to notice.

I looked at those paws. I looked at that posture. I looked at that face.
Bow tie. It was the only answer.

He is mine now — specifically and intentionally mine in the way that only rescued ferals can be. He chose me after everything. After the raptor gloves and the cat scratch fever and the back scratcher and the headboard incident at 3 AM. He went through all of that and still decided: her.

He is the proof that patience is a superpower. That stubborn love isn't stupidity — it's strategy. That some of the best things you'll ever have in your life start as feral cats on a security camera who want absolutely nothing to do with you.

He is my first feral rescue. My original case file. The one who started all of this.
Blue bow tie. Distinguished. Exactly as he should be.
