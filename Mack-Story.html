Chapter One

He Walked Like
He Had Somewhere
Important To Be.

That's the thing about this photo. That walk. Those ears already forward. That bobtail already held high. He had never been loved yet — not properly, not permanently — and he was still moving through the world like he was expected somewhere.

We should have known then. That kind of confidence doesn't get installed. It comes factory-loaded. You either have it or you don't. Mack has it in abundance.

He came in with his siblings — Diva, Astro, Cosmo — Nova's whole litter. A tiny striped chaos agent in a parking lot, nub already present, personality already fully formed, waiting for someone to catch up to him.

He didn't need rehabilitating. He didn't need convincing. He just needed a house large enough to contain him. We weren't fully prepared.

"He never had a before. He just had a beginning — and he hit the ground running."

The before. Bobtail already present. Walk already confident. Nobody warned us.
Day One. The whole crew. Diva is already judging. Astro is already hungry. Mack is just... Mack.
Chapter Two

Brad Named Him
After His
Grandpa.

That's not a small thing. You don't give a cat a name like that casually. You do it because you look at this small, ridiculous, enormous-personality creature — and you see something familiar in him. Someone you loved.

Brad's grandpa Mack was the extra-outgoing guy. The one who filled every room he walked into. The one who made people feel like the most important person there — not because he was performing it, but because that's just how he was built.

Brad saw that in this cat on day one. The name was decided before the second day.

And if you've ever watched Mack enter a room — 13 pounds of Maine Coon bobtail strutting in like he received an engraved invitation — you understand immediately. The name was not a stretch. The name was a statement of fact.

Grandpa Mack filled every room he walked into. So does this one.

Already filling the frame. Already himself. From the very beginning.
Chapter Three

I Never Thought
I'd Childproof
A House For A Cat.

The mini fridge in the office was small. Unassuming. A Popeyes cup in the door, some drinks, the usual. Not something you think to secure. Not something you imagine requires a lock.

Mack was the first creature outside of me to open it.

Let that sit for a moment. The first creature. Not the second, not "one of the cats." The first. Before Brad. Before any reasonable adult human with opposable thumbs who might have a legitimate reason to open a fridge.

Mack got there first. Mack always gets there first. That is his gift and our ongoing logistical challenge.

The childproofing began that day. We now live in a house secured against a 13-pound tabby with problem-solving instincts, zero impulse control, and absolutely no concept of "that's not yours." He remains deeply unbothered by all of it.

⚠ Incident Report — The Fridge

Location: Home office. Small mini fridge. Popeyes cup in the door.

Suspect: Mack. 13 lbs. Bobtail. No remorse.

Method: Unknown. Still under investigation.

Outcome: Childproofing installed. Cat remains at large.

Suspect's response: None. He was already on the couch.

This was not his last incident. This was his first documented one.
Chapter Four

He Knows
Before You
Say A Word.

Here is the thing about Mack that nobody tells you when you adopt a cat with this much personality: underneath all of it — the fridge incidents, the 13-pound body blocks, the butt whack demands — is a creature who is genuinely, profoundly tuned into the people he loves.

Get tearful. Just try it. Before you've said anything, before you've reached for a tissue — Astro is on your lap. And Mack is in your face. Literally. That large Maine Coon face, two inches from yours, doing absolutely nothing except being completely present.

He doesn't meow at you. He doesn't do anything dramatic. He just shows up and refuses to leave until you're okay. That's the whole move. He shows up.

And when it's Mack who needs love? He finds you. He plants that 13-pound frame directly in your path, sits down like he has nowhere else to be, and waits. Butt whacks on demand. Nub wagging when you deliver. The transaction is simple and non-negotiable.

He is the golden retriever of this household. Science agrees. Maine Coons are famous for dog-like loyalty. Mack did not read the breed description. He just lived it.

"You can't fake being upset around Mack. He already knows. He was already on his way."

This is the face that shows up when you need it most. Zero warning. Zero agenda.
The side-eye that says: I see you, I'm coming, deal with it.

"Where's My Babies?"

Yelled from the top of the stairs.
One voice. Two words. Maximum volume.

The boy who comes running. Every single time.

Mack flies up the stairs like a herd of elephants. That's not poetic license — that's an accurate acoustic description of what 13 pounds of Maine Coon bobtail sounds like at full speed on hardwood stairs.

Brad's other cats — his cats, the ones he feeds and loves and talks to all day — do not move. They look up, perhaps. They consider. They go back to what they were doing.

Mack comes running. Every time. Without fail. Like his name was called.
Like this is what he's been waiting for.
Like she's his person and everyone else should know it.

Brad is deeply unimpressed that his own cats will do this for Merry and not for him.
Brad has not said this out loud. His face says it for him.
Chapter Five

He Follows
Diva Like
She's The Law.

For all his confidence, all his himbo chaos energy, all his fridge-opening, stair-thundering, face-planting devotion — Mack has one person in this house whose authority he recognizes completely and without question.

His sister. Diva.

She leads. He follows. No discussion, no negotiation, no attempt to assert dominance. When Diva moves, Mack monitors. When Diva decides something, Mack defers. She is the CEO of Casa Grant and he has accepted this arrangement entirely.

It is honestly the most endearing thing about him. This 13-pound, fridge-opening, Maine Coon golden retriever — fully capable of doing whatever he wants, whenever he wants — chooses to take his cues from a tabby who wears a rhinestone collar and hunts felt grasshoppers.

She has earned it. And Mack, for all his chaos, knows quality when he sees it.

The eyes that watch Diva's every move. Loyal. Attentive. Completely unbothered by his own devotion.
